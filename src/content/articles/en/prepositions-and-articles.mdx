## Prepositions: think “relation”, not translation

### in / on / at (place)

- **at**: point-like locations: _at the bus stop_, _at the conference._
- **on**: surfaces, streets, floors: _on the table_, _on Main Street._
- **in**: enclosed areas/volumes: _in the lab_, _in the appendix._

### in / on / at (time)

- **at**: clock points: _at 9:15._
- **on**: days/dates: _on Tuesday_, _on 3 May._
- **in**: longer periods: _in 2024_, _in July_, _in the morning_ (but note locale-specific preferences).

### by / with / for (three frequent confusions)

- **by**: agent in passives, means/method, deadline: _by the authors_, _by hand_, _by Friday._
- **with**: accompaniment/instrument presented as co-participant: _cut with scissors._
- **for**: purpose/benefit/duration-for-purpose: _for comparison_, _for two years_ (sense-dependent).

## Articles: definite vs indefinite vs zero

### a/an

Use with **first mention** or **non-specific** reference in singular: _We ran a pilot study._

### the

Use when both speaker and reader can **identify** the referent (previous mention, uniqueness, shared knowledge): _The results replicate earlier work._

### zero article

Common with **plural generics** and many **uncountable generics**: _Students often underestimate revision time._ / _Information is noisy._

## Academic micro-patterns

- **the + noun** when the text has **narrowed** the reference: _The model assumes independence._
- **a/an** when introducing a **new** instance: _We propose a Bayesian hierarchical model._

## Five-minute practice

Pick 10 noun phrases from your last paragraph. For each, ask: **first mention or identifiable?** **countable?** **generic or specific?** Then check the preposition’s **relation** (place/time/means/agent), not the Portuguese or Spanish gloss.
