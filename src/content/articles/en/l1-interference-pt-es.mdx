## Articles: the zero-article habit

Portuguese and Spanish can omit articles where English needs **a/the** or where **plural generics** behave differently.

Quick fix: classify the noun phrase:

- **countable singular** usually needs **a/the**: _We introduce **a** method._
- **generic plural** often uses **zero article**: _Methods vary by domain._

## Prepositions: never one-to-one

High-error mappings include **em**/**en** → _in/on/at_, **por/para** → _for/by/to_, and **com**/**con** → _with_.

Fix: memorize **collocations** (_depend on_, _interested in_, _focus on_) instead of “the meaning of _in_”.

## False friends and cognates

Cognates help vocabulary but sabotage **register** and **collocation**:

- _actual_ ≠ _atual/actual_ “current” → use **_current_**, **_present_**.
- _pretend_ ≠ _pretender_ “intend” → use **_intend/plan to_**.

Keep a personal **false-friend list** from your own revised drafts.

## Word order: adjective placement

English is predominantly **Noun–Adjective** only in fixed patterns; most adjectives precede nouns. Portuguese/Spanish post-noun adjectives easily transfer:

> _a solution feasible_ → _a **feasible** solution_

## A practical workflow

When a teacher marks an error, tag it: **article**, **preposition**, **word order**, **cognate**, **tense/aspect**. Revisit one tag per week with 10 fresh sentences you wrote yourself—not textbook drills.
