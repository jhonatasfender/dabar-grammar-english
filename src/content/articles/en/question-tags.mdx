## Canonical form

_You’re **coming**, **aren’t you**?_ — **positive** statement → **negative** tag.

## Auxiliary matching

Use the **same auxiliary** as the anchor (_have_, _do_, _will_, modals): _She **hasn’t** left**, **has** she**?_

## Rising vs falling intonation

- **Rising:** genuine **uncertainty** or **invitation**.
- **Falling:** **statement of expectation** (_Nice day**, **isn’t it\*\*?_) — more **rhetorical**.

## Special cases

- **Let’s …, shall we?**
- **Imperatives …, will you / won’t you?** (force differences)
- **I am …, aren’t I?** — suppletive tag (non-standard _amn’t I_ in some dialects)

## Invariant tags in informal English

_…, **innit**?_, _…, **right**?_ — sociolectal; avoid in formal writing.

## Quick checklist

1. Is polarity **reversed** for a neutral tag?
2. Does the auxiliary **match** the finite of the anchor?
3. Does intonation match **information-seeking** vs **stance**?
