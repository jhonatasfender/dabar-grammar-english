## Simple: result or whole event

_We’ve **finished** the migration._ (done)

## Continuous: process still recent or ongoing

_We’ve **been finishing** the migration — one service remains._ (stresses **effort / stretch**)

Some verbs **disprefer** continuous (_know_, _believe_) except in special pragmatic shifts.

## Quantity and repetition readings

- _I’ve **read** three papers today._ (counted achievements — simple)
- _I’ve **been reading** papers all morning._ (activity stretch — continuous)

## Implicature of temporary states

_He’s **been acting** strangely._ may implicate **recent** behaviour without claiming permanence.

## Quick checklist

1. Is the point **completion** or **process span**?
2. Does the verb **allow** progressive aspect?
3. Would continuous wrongly suggest **incompleteness** in a results section?
