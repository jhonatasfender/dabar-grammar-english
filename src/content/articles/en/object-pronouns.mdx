## Subject vs object (core paradigm)

| Subject | Object   |
| ------- | -------- |
| I       | **me**   |
| you     | you      |
| he      | **him**  |
| she     | **her**  |
| it      | it       |
| we      | **us**   |
| they    | **them** |

Use **object** forms whenever the pronoun is **not** the finite clause subject in canonical declaratives.

## After verbs (direct and indirect objects)

- _She emailed **me**._ (direct object)
- _Pass **him** the salt._ / _Pass the salt to **him**._ (indirect object)
- _We’ll ship **it** tomorrow._
- _They invited **us**._
- _I didn’t tell **them**._

## After prepositions (mandatory object case)

English prepositions take **object** pronouns, never subject forms:

- _Between **you and me**, …_ (not _between you and I_ in standard usage)
- _This isn’t about **her**._
- _Look at **it**._
- _They left without **us**._
- _I’m tired of arguing with **them**._

## Coordinated pronoun + noun

- **Subject:** _**My colleague and I** submitted the report._
- **Object:** _The editor thanked **my colleague and me**._

Tip: temporarily drop the other conjoin — _**I** submitted_ vs _thanked **me**_.

## _Her_: object vs possessive

- **Object:** _I saw **her**._
- **Possessive determiner:** _**Her** draft_ — same word, different function (not an object pronoun in that use).

## Link to reflexives

Reflexives compound object forms with _-self/-selves_ (_myself_, _themselves_). See the article on **reflexive pronouns** for coargument and emphatic uses.

## Quick checklist

1. Is the pronoun **subject** or **complement/prepositional**?
2. After **any** preposition, is the form **me/him/her/us/them** (or **you/it**)?
3. In coordination, did you test **I vs me** by **ellipsis**?
