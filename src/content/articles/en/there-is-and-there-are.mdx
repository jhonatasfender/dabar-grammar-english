## Basic function

_There **is** a flaw in the argument._ — _There_ is **expletive** (no locative meaning); the **true subject** is _a flaw_.

## Agreement: formal vs spoken

- Formal: _There **are** two reasons and one caveat._
- Informal: _There’s two reasons_ — widely heard; avoid in edited academic English unless dialogue.

## Lists and “first NP” rule

When the first NP is singular but later NPs are plural, restructure: _There **is** a problem: several reviewers disagree._ or front the plural: _There **are** several reviewers and one editor._

## Advanced: not locative _there_

Do not confuse with locative adverb _there_: _There is the lab_ (pointing) vs _There is a lab on campus_ (existence).

## Alternatives for density

- _Two issues **exist**._ — denser, formal.
- _**It** is necessary **that**…_ — extraposition, different information structure.

## Five-minute practice

Rewrite five existential sentences for **journal-style** agreement; where awkward, switch to _exist_ or front the subject.
