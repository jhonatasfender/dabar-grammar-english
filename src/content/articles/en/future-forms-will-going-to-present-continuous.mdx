## _Will_ — prediction, promise, on-the-spot decision

- Spontaneous commitment: _I’ll send the diff tonight._
- General prediction without emphasising prior plan: _This will hurt readability._

## _Be going to_ — evidence and “already mentally underway”

- **Perceptual evidence:** _Look at those clouds — it’s going to rain._
- **Prior intention** (often already prepared): _I’m going to defend the nested model._

Overlap with **will** is massive; **going to** foregrounds **antecedent basis** (plan, evidence).

## Present continuous — diary futures

- **Fixed personal schedule:** _I’m flying to Lisbon on Thursday._
- Implicates **external booking** or **shared knowledge** — less natural for detached predictions about third parties unless a schedule exists.

## Present simple for timetabled events

- **Institutional schedules:** _The train leaves at 06:12._

## Choosing in production

Ask:

1. Am I stating a **bare prediction** or citing **grounds**?
2. Is the event **on a shared calendar**?
3. Is this an **instant speech-act commitment** (**will**)?

## Quick checklist

1. Does **going to** reflect **visible evidence** or **pre-existing intention**?
2. Is progressive limited to **arranged** / **mutually accessible** futures?
3. Have you avoided **will** for **already arranged** diary events where progressive is more native?
