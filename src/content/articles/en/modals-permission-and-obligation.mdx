## Permission

- **can** — neutral/informal permission: _You **can** leave early._
- **could** — more **tentative/polite**: _**Could** I interrupt for a second?_
- **may** — formal **authorization** (declining in casual speech but alive in rules): _Candidates **may** consult one page of notes._

## Obligation and strong recommendation

- **must** — speaker-strong or rule-internalised necessity: _You **must** anonymise the data._
- **have to** — **external** requirement: _We **have to** follow IRB procedures._
- **need to** — **goal-oriented** necessity: _You **need to** fix the leak before release._
- **should / ought to** — **non-legal** expectation or advice.

## Prohibition

- **must not / may not** — normative ban (_You **must not** store passwords in plain text._)
- **can’t** — practical or rule-governed inability/permission block in informal registers.

## Softening deontics

_Perhaps we **should** revisit the assumption_; _You **might want to** double-check the licence._

## Quick checklist

1. Is the source of obligation **internal** (guideline) or **external** (law, timetable)?
2. Does **permission** need **formal** (**may**) or **colloquial** (**can**) colouring?
3. Does negation target **permission**, **obligation**, or **epistemic** space — avoid ambiguity.
