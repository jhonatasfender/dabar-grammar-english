## Future continuous (_will be_ + _-ing_)

- **Core use:** activity **in progress** around a future time or within a future window.
- **Pragmatics:** softens **fixed-time** claims (_I’ll be working at nine_ vs _I’ll work at nine_).

_At 10 p.m. UTC I’ll still be running the batch job._

## Future perfect (_will have_ + past participle)

- **Core use:** state or event **completed before** some **future orientation point**.
- Often pairs with **by**, **before**, or a **when**-clause whose main event is later.

_By Friday we will have submitted the camera-ready version._

## Contrast with simple future

- **Simple future:** default **prediction / decision / offer**.
- **Future continuous:** **interior view** of an interval; resists “punctual” readings unless coerced contextually.
- **Future perfect:** **backwards-looking** from a future vantage — “already done by then”.

## Common learner traps

1. Using future perfect for **vague promises** without a **reference point** — add an explicit anchor.
2. Confusing **will have been -ing** (future perfect continuous) with plain future perfect — the former stresses **duration up to** the point.

## Quick checklist

1. Are you describing **mid-activity** at \(t\) (continuous) or **closure before** \(t\) (perfect)?
2. Is there a **clear temporal landmark** for the perfect?
3. Does progressive **politeness / mitigation** fit the speech act?
