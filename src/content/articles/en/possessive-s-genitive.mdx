## Core patterns

- **Possession:** _the lab’s equipment_
- **Authorship / origin:** _Chomsky’s proposal_
- **Measure / part:** _a day’s work_

## Subjective vs objective ambiguity

_**John’s** photograph_ can mean **John took it** (subjective) or **depicts John** (objective). Disambiguate with prepositions: _a photograph **of** John_, _a photograph **by** John_.

## Classifying genitive

_A **children’s** hospital_ — hospital **for** children, not owned by a specific child.

## Group genitives

_**someone else’s** results_, _**the CEO of the firm’s** decision_ — acceptable in speech; in formal writing, rephrase for clarity: _the decision **by** the CEO of the firm_.

## _Of_ vs _’s_ for weight

Long inanimates with postmodifiers often read better with _of_: _the **probability** of observing an effect **under** null conditions_ rather than stacking _’s_.

## Five-minute practice

Take five NPs with _’s_; for each, try an _of_-paraphrase and a _by/for_-paraphrase; keep the clearest for your genre.
