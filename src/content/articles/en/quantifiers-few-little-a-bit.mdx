## Count vs mass

- **Count plural:** _few problems_ / _a few problems_
- **Mass:** _little time_ / _a little time_

Do not use _few_ with prototypical mass nouns (\*few water).

## Polarity and stance

- _Few reviewers objected._ — implies **not many did** (neutral-to-negative expectation).
- _A few reviewers objected._ — neutral report of **some** objectors.

## _Quite a few_ = “a considerable number”

This is **not** intensified scarcity; it patterns with “many”: _We received quite a few corrections._

## _A bit of_ and informal approximators

_A bit of noise_ softens mass reference; in formal prose, _some_, _a degree of_, or restructuring may be safer.

## Five-minute practice

Rewrite five sentences swapping _few_ ↔ _a few_ and _little_ ↔ _a little_; note how **expectedness** and **politeness** shift.
