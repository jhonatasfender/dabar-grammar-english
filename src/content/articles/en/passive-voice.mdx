## Canonical passive

_The issue **was** **fixed** **by** the on-call engineer._ — patient **subject**, optional **by**-phrase.

## Why passivise?

- **Patient topicalisation** across sentences.
- **Agent unknown** or **irrelevant**.
- **Delicacy** or **policy** (sometimes criticised as “agent deletion”).

## Get-passives

_He **got** **fired**._ — more **colloquial**, sometimes **adversative** nuance.

## Stative vs actional participles

_The door **was** **closed**._ may be **state** (copular-like) or **event** — context disambiguates.

## Long passive and coordination

Keep **auxiliary** scope clear in **coordination**: _The tests **were** designed and **run** by two teams._ (avoid dangling ellipsis).

## Quick checklist

1. Would active voice make **agency** clearer **without** clutter?
2. Is the **by**-phrase needed for **fairness / reproducibility**?
3. Does the passive string remain **readable** (avoid stacked prepositional passives)?
