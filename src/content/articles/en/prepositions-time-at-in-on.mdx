## _at_: precise time

_at 9:15_, _at noon_, _at midnight_, _at lunchtime_ (treated as point).

## _on_: days and dated occasions

_on Monday_, _on 3 May_, _on the day of the defence._

## _in_: periods and containers

_in July_, _in 2024_, _in the morning_ — but note: _**on** Monday morning_ (named day + part).

## Weekend variation

- AmE: _on the weekend_
- BrE: _at the weekend_

Pick the variety you are targeting; mixed usage in one document looks inconsistent.

## _in_ + time-to-event

_In two weeks_ can mean **after a two-week interval**; disambiguate with _within two weeks_ or _two weeks from now_ in contracts and methods.

## Five-minute practice

Write the same schedule in **BrE** and **AmE** preposition choices; flag every divergence.
