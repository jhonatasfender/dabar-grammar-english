## General ability: _could_

- _I **could** read Latin fluently as a student._ (capacity over a stretch of time)

## Single-episode success: _was/were able to_ / _managed to_

When the point is **succeeding at one event**, **be able to** (past) or **manage to** is often preferred:

- _The team **was able to** recover the corrupted shard._
- _She **managed to** reproduce the bug on staging._

**Managed to** adds **effort / overcoming obstacles**.

## Failure on a specific occasion

- **\_Couldn’t**_ works naturally for both inability and “didn’t manage”: \_I **couldn’t** unlock the file._

## Sensory and mental verbs

**Could** remains idiomatic for **bounded perceptions**: _I **could** hear the relay clicking._

## Quick checklist

1. Are you describing a **standing skill** or a **one-off outcome**?
2. Should the reader infer **difficulty overcome** (**managed to**)?
3. Does **could** wrongly suggest success when you mean only **hypothetical** ability? Clarify with context.
