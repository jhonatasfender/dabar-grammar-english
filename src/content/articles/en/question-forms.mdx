## Yes/no questions: move the first auxiliary

- _She **has** finished._ → _**Has** she finished?_
- _They **are** leaving._ → _**Are** they leaving?_

If the declarative has only a **lexical verb** in present/past simple, insert **do**: _You **agree**._ → _**Do** you agree?_

## Wh- questions: two slots

1. Wh-word (or phrase): _Which model…?_
2. Inversion after the Wh- constituent unless the Wh- is **subject**: _**Who** called?_ (no inversion — _who_ is subject).

## Indirect questions: no inversion

After _whether_, _if_, or Wh- in object position, use **statement order**: _Ask **whether they are** available_, not ✱_whether are they_.

## Negative questions and pragmatics

_Aren’t you ready?_ can signal **surprise** or **pressure**, not neutral information-seeking. In professional email, prefer neutral positives unless you intend that stance.

## Five-minute practice

Take five real questions from your correspondence. Mark each as **matrix** (standalone) vs **embedded**; fix any illegal inversion in subordinates.
