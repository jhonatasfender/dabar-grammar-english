## Adjective/adverb + enough

_The sample is **large** **enough**._  
_She ran **fast** **enough**._

## enough + noun

_We don’t have **enough** **memory**._ — **determiner-like** placement.

## enough … to-infinitive

_It’s **warm** **enough** **to** **melt** the ice._ — **result/sufficiency** link.

## not enough vs too …

- **not enough** — insufficiency under a norm.
- **too** — **excess** blocking a goal: _too **hot** **to** **touch**._

## enough of + NP

_Enough **of** **this** **noise**_ — partitive construction.

## Quick checklist

1. Is **enough** **after** the adjective/adverb but **before** the noun?
2. Does **to** introduce **result**, not purpose unrelated to sufficiency?
3. Have you avoided _enough good_ ordering mistakes?
