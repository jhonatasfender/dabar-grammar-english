## Copular structures

- **Identity / role:** _She is the PI on the grant._
- **Class / property:** _The solution is unstable._ — property reading, not an event.
- **Location as state:** _The keys are in the drawer._ — locative predicate adjective/PP.

## Temporary vs permanent: a pragmatic gradient

_Adjectives_ after _be_ can invite a **temporary** reading with progressive elsewhere, but _be_ itself stays simple: _He is tired_ vs _He is being difficult_ (the latter: deliberate behaviour, not pure state).

## Agreement and “closest noun” traps

In _The key to these doors **is** missing_, the copula agrees with **key**, not _doors_. In lists, decide whether the subject is **conjoined** (_Bread and butter **is** served_) or plural (_Bread and eggs **are** served_)—often idiom-dependent.

## _There is/are_ and agreement

Existentials agree with the **first post-verbal NP** in formal usage: _There **is** a problem and two issues._ In informal English, _there’s_ + plural is widespread; in careful academic prose, prefer full agreement or restructure.

## Five-minute practice

Find five sentences with _there is/are_ or _one of the X **is**_. Rewrite for formal agreement and check copula number against the **true subject**, not the nearest noun.
