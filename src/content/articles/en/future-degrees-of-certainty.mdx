## Adverbs and stance

- **High:** _definitely_, _certainly_, _clearly_ (watch overclaim risk).
- **Mid:** _probably_, _likely_ (adj/adv paradigms differ).
- **Low:** _possibly_, _perhaps_, _maybe_ (sentence vs medial positions vary).

_Position matters_: sentence adverbs scope differently from auxiliary-adjacent adverbs.

## Semi-modals: _be likely/unlikely to_, _be bound to_, _be due to_

- _The effect **is likely to** disappear after correction._
- _They **are due to** arrive before noon._ (scheduled expectation)

## Modal + adverb stacking

Avoid **redundant** certainty (_will definitely certainly_) — pick **one** strong marker unless emphatic **focus** is intended.

## Hedging in academic writing

Pair graded futures with **evidence** (_our simulations suggest_, _the trend is consistent with_).

## Quick checklist

1. Does the certainty marker **match** the strength of your evidence?
2. Are adverb **scopes** unambiguous?
3. Have you avoided **double hedging** that reads as vagueness (_might possibly_) unless intentional?
