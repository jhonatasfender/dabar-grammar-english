## Yes / no questions → _if_ / _whether_

Direct: _“**Are** you leaving?”_  
Reported: _She **asked** if / whether I **was** leaving._

**Do not** retain auxiliary–subject inversion in the embedded clause.

## Wh-questions

Direct: _“**Where** **did** they park?”_  
Reported: _He **asked** where they **had** parked._ (backshift under past matrix)

Wh-word stays **initial**; **no** _did_-support in the embedded finite clause.

## Embedded question as subject / object

_**Whether** the patch **is** safe **remains** unclear._ — noun-clause question without inversion.

## Politeness reporting

**Wonder** + **if/whether**: _I **wonder** whether we **should** rerun the tests._

## Quick checklist

1. After the wh-word, is the subject **before** the finite verb?
2. Is **if/whether** chosen without doubling the question auxiliary?
3. Does tense **harmonise** with a past reporting verb unless the content is timeless?
