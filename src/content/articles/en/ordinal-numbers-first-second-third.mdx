## What ordinals are for

- **Sequence / rank:** _the **first** hypothesis_, _she finished **third**_, _the **twenty-fifth** anniversary_.
- **Labels:** _**Third** Street_, _World War **II**_ (read “the Second”).
- **Partitions of a whole:** _a **third** of the sample_ — here _third_ is a **fraction** label; the grammar still patterns like an ordinal noun (_a fifth of the cake_).

They are **not** cardinals: compare _**three** experiments_ (how many) vs _the **third** experiment_ (which one in order).

## Irregular words (memorise these)

Most ordinals add _-th_ to the cardinal stem, but **these do not**:

| Cardinal | Ordinal                       | Figure |
| -------- | ----------------------------- | ------ |
| one      | **first**                     | 1st    |
| two      | **second**                    | 2nd    |
| three    | **third**                     | 3rd    |
| five     | **fifth** (not ✱*fiveth*)     | 5th    |
| eight    | **eighth** (one _t_)          | 8th    |
| nine     | **ninth** (drop the _e_)      | 9th    |
| twelve   | **twelfth** (not ✱*twelveth*) | 12th   |

Everything else in the 4–20 band is regular: _fourth_, _sixth_, _seventh_, _tenth_, _fourteenth_, _twentieth_, etc.

## Figures: _st_, _nd_, _rd_, _th_

In **digits**, ordinals use a suffix on the **whole number**:

- **1** → **1st** (_first_), **21** → **21st**, **101** → **101st** — same pattern as “first”.
- **2** → **2nd**, **22** → **22nd**, **402** → **402nd**.
- **3** → **3rd**, **23** → **23rd**.
- **4–20** and most other endings → **4th**, **5th**, **11th**, **20th**, **24th**, **100th**.

**Exception (often missed by L2 writers):** **11, 12, 13** always take **_th_**, because the spoken forms end in _-eleven_, _-twelve_, _-thirteen_: **11th**, **12th**, **13th** — never ✱11st, ✱12nd, ✱13rd.

In **large compounds**, only the **last** chunk is ordinal in form: **421st** (_four hundred and twenty-**first**_), **5,111th** (_five thousand, one hundred and eleventh_).

## Hyphens in full words (21–99)

When you spell out compounds, the **tens word stays cardinal**, the **units word is ordinal**, with a **hyphen**: _twenty-**first**_, _thirty-**second**_, _ninety-**ninth**_.

## _The_ + ordinal + noun

With a **specific** referent, **_the_ + ordinal** is the default: _**the first** author listed_, _**the second** phase_. ✱*A second phase* is odd unless you mean “another phase” (indefinite **another**, not ordinal “second in list”).

Predicative order: _She was **first**._ / _This model came **third**._ — no article.

## Dates and regnal style

- **US-style dates:** _May **5th**_ / _May **5**_ — often read “May **fifth**”.
- **UK-style:** _**the 5th of May**_ / _**5 May**_.

**Monarchs and popes:** _Elizabeth **II**_, _John **XXIII**_ — read with ordinals: “Elizabeth the **Second**”, “John the **twenty-third**”.

## Five-minute practice

Write the ordinal **in words** and **in figures**: 11, 21, 22, 23, 111, 212. Say aloud why **11th** is not ✱11st.
