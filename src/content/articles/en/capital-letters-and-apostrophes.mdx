## Capital letters

- **Sentence-initial** words.
- **Proper names**, **nationalities**, **language names**.
- **Titles** when part of a name (_Professor **Chen**_) but not generic (_the professor_).

## Apostrophe possessives

- _the student**’s** draft_ (singular)
- _the students**’** drafts_ (plural ending in -s)

Joint possession: _Sam and **Alex’s** server_ (shared) vs _Sam’s and Alex’s roles_ (split).

## Contractions

- **it’s** = it is / it has
- **its** = possessive determiner (**no** apostrophe)

## Lets vs let’s

**Let’s** = let us; **lets** = third-person present of _let_.

## Quick checklist

1. Is the capital marking **grammar** (sentence) or **lexical** (proper)?
2. Does the apostrophe attach to the **possessor NP**, not the head noun randomly?
3. Have you avoided **greengrocer’s apostrophe** for ordinary plurals?
