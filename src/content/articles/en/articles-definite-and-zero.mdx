## Plural generics

_Students **often** procrastinate._ — class as a whole, not a specific set.

## Mass nouns in general statements

_Information **is** asymmetric._ — mass noun without article in the generic reading.

## When _the_ particularises

_**The** students in cohort B underperformed._ — delimited set, identifiable in context.

## “Kind” readings with _the_ + singular count

_**The** tiger is endangered._ — species-level statement (formal); _A tiger is a large cat_ introduces **token-type** differently.

## Overuse from L1 (PT/ES)

Portuguese/Spanish often allow definite article where English uses **zero** for abstract mass generics—compare discipline-specific collocations rather than translating article-for-article.

## Five-minute practice

In one methods paragraph, mark each NP as **generic mass**, **generic plural**, or **specific**; remove or add _the_ where the reading flips incorrectly.
