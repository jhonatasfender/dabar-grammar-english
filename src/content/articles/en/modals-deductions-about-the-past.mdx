## Epistemic past: the perfect infinitive

- **must have** — strong retrospective inference: _She **must have** pulled the old branch._
- **may/might/could have** — past possibility not ruled out: _It **might have** been a caching issue._
- **can’t/couldn’t have** — denial of a past scenario: _He **can’t have** seen the final prompt._

## _Could have_ + past participle (layered)

1. **Open past possibility** (epistemic): _You **could have** been right._
2. **Unrealised past ability/opportunity** (sometimes mildly critical): _You **could have** warned us._

Context and stress disambiguate.

## Avoid collapsing epistemic and deontic

**Had to have** is not a standard parallel to **must have**; prefer **must have** or **had to** depending on intended meaning.

## Academic prose

Anchor each modal in **data**: _Given the timestamps, the job **must have** started after 02:00 UTC._

## Quick checklist

1. Is the modal scoping over a **past event/state**?
2. Does **could have** mean **possible then** or **missed opportunity**?
3. Is **can’t have** a **denial of possibility**, not just low plausibility?
