## Hedging without weakening

**Hedges** (_may_, _might_, _could_, _seems_, _tends to_) manage **commitment** to claims. They fail when they stack without evidence.

Pattern: **hedge + reason + pointer to data**:

> The effect **may** be nonlinear; **however**, the confidence intervals widen below _n_ = 30.

## Reporting verbs and attribution

Choose verbs that match **evidential strength**:

- **show/suggest/indicate**: stronger (use carefully).
- **argue/claim/propose**: frames a position.
- **observe/report/find**: neutral description of a study.

Keep **tense** consistent with your discipline’s conventions for primary vs secondary literature.

## Cohesion: old-before-new

Readers track topics left-to-right. Prefer **known information early**, **new information late** in the clause:

> **This estimator** is biased. **The bias** grows when labels are noisy.

## Voice: passive is a tool, not a default

Use passive when the **action** matters more than the **agent** (_participants were randomly assigned_). Avoid passive chains that hide responsibility (_it was decided_).

## One revision pass (10 minutes)

Highlight every **claim sentence**. For each, mark: **Who did it?** **How sure are you?** **What evidence supports it?** Fix grammar where those three answers are unclear.
