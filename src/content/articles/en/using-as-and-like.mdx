## like + NP

_She runs **like** the wind._ — **resemblance**.

## like + gerund (informal)

_Feels **like** **raining**._ — conversational; some editors prefer **as if** + clause.

## as + NP (role / function)

_She works **as** a mentor._ — **capacity**, not mere similarity.

## Clausal frames

- **as + finite clause** in formal comparatives: _**As** we noted earlier, …_
- **as if / as though** for hypothetical manner: _He acted **as if** nothing **had** happened._

## He’s like / It’s like (discourse)

Quotative **like** is **spoken** narrative — avoid in formal prose.

## Quick checklist

1. Are you marking **role** (**as**) or **resemblance** (**like**)?
2. Is a **clause** needed? Prefer **as if** over bare **like** in careful writing.
3. Have you avoided **like** as subordinating conjunction in academic registers?
