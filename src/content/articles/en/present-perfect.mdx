## Resultative reading

_I’ve **lost** the keys — I can’t open the lab._ (past event, **present consequence**)

## Indefinite past (no fixed time adverbial)

_I’ve **read** that monograph_, but avoid pairing with **yesterday**-style anchors — use **simple past**.

## Continuative (state verbs / for–since)

_We’ve **lived** here since 2019._ (still true)

## News / hot-news perfect

_The board **has** just **announced** the merger._ (recent, relevant **now**)

## Contrast with past simple

- **Past simple:** narrated **then**, often with **calendar time**.
- **Present perfect:** **bridges** to **now**; resists **finished time adverbs** like _in 2010_ unless framed as **experience** carefully.

## Quick checklist

1. Is **now** part of the **truth conditions**?
2. Are you avoiding **yesterday / last week** with the perfect (unless dialectal tolerance)?
3. Does the verb allow **continuative** reading, or force **eventive** punctuality?
