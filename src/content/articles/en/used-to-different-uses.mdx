## _used to + infinitive_ (past habit/state)

- _I **used to** smoke._ (no longer)
- Negation: _I **didn’t use to** care._ / _I **used not to** care._ (rarer)

There is **no present** _use to_ parallel — use **usually**, **tend to**, or **will** for present habits.

## _be / get used to + noun / -ing_

- **Accustomed:** _She **is used to** long compile times._
- **Process:** _We **are getting used to** the new linter rules._

The **to** here is **prepositional** — what follows must be **NP** or **gerund**.

## Passive _be used to + infinitive_ (different construction)

- _This modal **is used to** express permission._ (purpose — **not** habit)

## Quick checklist

1. After **used to**, is the next word a **plain infinitive**?
2. After **be used to**, is the complement **-ing** or a **noun** (accustomed sense)?
3. Have you avoided importing **present** _use to_ for habits?
