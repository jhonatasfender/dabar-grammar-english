## Count nouns: individuated units

Take **a/an**, plural **-s**, and numerals: _three hypotheses_, _an outlier_.

## Mass (uncountable) nouns: substance, abstraction, aggregate

No plural *-s* in the mass reading: _much evidence_, _little friction_. Use **partitives** for bounded quantities: _two pieces of evidence_, _a body of work_.

## Dual-life nouns

- _Paper_ (material) vs _papers_ (documents).
- _Time_ (duration mass) vs _times_ (occasions).

## _Data_: usage split

In many fields, _data **is**_ (mass) competes with _data **are**_ (plural of _datum_). Pick a **house style** and stay consistent; do not mix within one paragraph without reason.

## L1 interference (PT/ES)

Portuguese and Spanish often omit articles where English requires **a determiner** or allows zero with plurals—pair this article with [Articles: _a_, _an_, _the_](/en/articles-a-an-the).

## Five-minute practice

List ten nouns from your field. Mark each **count**, **mass**, or **both**; for mass nouns, write an acceptable **partitive** paraphrase.
