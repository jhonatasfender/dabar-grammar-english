## Gradability as a semantic scale

- **Open scale:** _cold → very cold → freezing_
- **Endpoint adjectives:** _excellent_, _tiny_ — intensifiers shift **strength** but do not behave like midpoints.

## Non-gradable families

- **Absolutes / limits:** _unique_, _dead_, _full_ (many allow **emphatic** colloquial intensification — register-sensitive).
- **Classifying:** _atomic_, _digital_, _main_ — resist _very_ in careful usage.

## Intensifier selection

- **Maximisers** with non-gradable bases: _absolutely **fantastic**_, _utterly **wrong**_
- **Boosters** with gradable bases: _very **tall**_, _rather **cold**_

## Mitigators

_a bit_, _slightly_, _fairly_ target **mid-scale** positions and clash with **absolute** readings.

## Quick checklist

1. Does the adjective presuppose a **scale**?
2. Is your intensifier a **booster** or a **maximiser**?
3. Have you avoided _very unique_ in **formal** registers?
