## _just_

Typically **medial** after auxiliary: _I’ve **just** pushed the tag._

## _already_

Often **affirmative** perfects; can sound **impatient** in questions: _Have you **already** merged without review?_

## _yet_

- **Questions / negatives**: _She hasn’t replied **yet**._ / _Have they landed **yet**?_
- Avoid redundant _yet_ in plain affirmatives.

## _still_

- _I **still** haven’t heard back._ (persistent non-completion)
- With progressive perfect: _I’m **still** working on it_ vs _I’ve **still** been waiting_ — subtle **stance** differences.

## Register and ellipsis

Informal **just** ~ **recently**; **yet** placement at **end** is stereotypic in many varieties.

## Quick checklist

1. Does **yet** appear in a **non-negative** assertion without special framing?
2. Does **already** carry unwanted **surprise/pressure** in a polite request?
3. Is **just** signalling **hot news** rather than simple past narration?
