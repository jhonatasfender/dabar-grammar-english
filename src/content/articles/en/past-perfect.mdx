## Core meaning: “earlier past”

_By the time we landed, the team **had already deployed** the hotfix._

The **past simple** carries the **main storyline**; the **past perfect** fills **backstory**.

## With time clauses

- _After she **had finished** calibrating, she ran the benchmark._
- In informal registers, speakers sometimes use **simple past** in both clauses when order is **clear from logic** — edited prose often keeps the perfect for **precision**.

## Counterfactual anchor

Third conditional and related patterns: _If I **had known**, I would have acted differently._

## Stative verbs

Stative **had known / had believed** is natural; avoid overusing progressive unless emphasising **temporary state**.

## Quick checklist

1. Is there a **second past reference** the reader must order before?
2. Would simple past create **temporal ambiguity**?
3. In **if**-clauses, is the situation **closed and counterfactual**?
