## _at_: schematic point

_at the bus stop_, _at the intersection_, _at the conference venue_ — you care about **being there**, not interior geometry.

## _on_: surface, route-like lines, floors

_on the table_, _on the wall_, _on Main Street_ (US), _on the second floor_.

## _in_: enclosed region / interior frame

_in the lab_, _in the city centre_, _in the appendix_ (metaphorical container).

## Construal shifts

- _She is **in** the office_ (inside the room) vs _She is **at** the office_ (workplace as a point on her schedule).

## Advanced: institutions

_Be **at** university_ (BrE) vs _be **in** college_ (AmE tendencies vary) — check target variety; do not translate directly from Portuguese _na faculdade_ without checking collocations.

## Five-minute practice

Describe the same three locations twice: once emphasising **interior geometry** (_in_), once emphasising **appointment/presence** (_at_).
