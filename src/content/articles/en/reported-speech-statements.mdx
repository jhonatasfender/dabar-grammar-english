## Basic shift pattern

Direct: _“We **are** ready.”_  
Reported: _They **said** (that) they **were** ready._

Backshift is **tempted** when the matrix clause is **past** (_said_, _told_, _claimed_).

## When backshift is optional

General truths: _She said (that) water **boils** at 100 °C._ — both present and past remain acceptable depending on **perspective**.

## Pronoun and deictic adjustment

**I → he/she**, **here → there**, **tomorrow → the next day** — align with the **reporter’s** coordinate system unless you retain **direct speech flavour**.

## Reporting verbs beyond _say_

- **tell** needs an object: _She **told** us (that) …_
- **claim, argue, suggest** add **stance**; some trigger **subjunctive** in formal registers (_insist that he **be** present_ — rare in contemporary British English).

## That-clause omission

Conversational English often drops **_that_**; dense prose may retain it for **scope** clarity.

## Quick checklist

1. Is the matrix verb **past**, making backshift **natural**?
2. Does the content remain **valid at reporting time** without shift?
3. Are **pronouns** and **time adverbials** consistent with the reporter?
