For **subject vs object** forms (_I/me_, _they/them_), see the article **object pronouns** (`object-pronouns`).

## True reflexives

_She **taught** **herself** Python._ — **subject** and **reflexive** coindexed.

## Emphatic

_I **myself** ran the benchmark._ — **appositive** reinforcement, not object of _ran_.

## Prepositional reflexives

_He bought a ticket **for** **himself**._ — **Benefactive** coargument.

## Lexicalised reflexives

- _**Behave** **yourself**._
- _**Pride** **yourself** **on** …_

## Avoid over-generation

_The team discussed the issue among themselves_ is fine; _He cut himself the cake_ needs **benefactive/dative** justification — often **rephrase**.

## Quick checklist

1. Is there a **local** antecedent in the right **argument position**?
2. Is this **emphatic** apposition rather than object?
3. Is the reflexive **lexically** required by the verb?
